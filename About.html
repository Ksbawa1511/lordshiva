About Lord Shiva

Discover the divine essence and profound wisdom of the Supreme Being in Shaivism

Divine Origins

Lord Shiva, also known as Mahadeva, is one of the principal deities of Hinduism and the Supreme Being in Shaivism. His origins trace back to pre-Vedic times, evolving from various ancient deities including the Rigvedic storm god Rudra.

Sacred Attributes

  • The third eye (Trinetra) - symbol of wisdom and destruction
  • The crescent moon - representing time and consciousness
  • The serpent Vasuki - symbol of cosmic energy
  • The trident (Trishula) - representing the three gunas
  • The damaru drum - symbol of cosmic sound

Cosmic Role

Within the Hindu trinity (Trimurti), Shiva is known as "The Destroyer," but this destruction is not negative - it represents transformation, change, and the dissolution of illusion. He destroys to recreate, maintaining the cosmic cycle.

Divine Functions

  • Destruction of ignorance and ego
  • Transformation of consciousness
  • Dissolution of the universe
  • Liberation of souls (moksha)
  • Maintenance of cosmic order

Spiritual Significance

Shiva represents the ultimate reality beyond all forms and names. He is the Adiyogi (first yogi) who taught yoga to humanity, the Nataraja (lord of dance) who performs the cosmic dance, and the supreme ascetic who meditates eternally.

Core Qualities

  • Detachment and renunciation
  • Meditation and contemplation
  • Compassion and benevolence
  • Fearlessness and courage
  • Wisdom and knowledge

Sacred Family

Shiva's divine family includes his consort Parvati (Shakti), their sons Ganesha and Kartikeya, and their daughter Ashokasundari. Together they represent the perfect balance of masculine and feminine energies.

Divine Family

  • Parvati - The divine mother and energy
  • Ganesha - Remover of obstacles
  • Kartikeya - God of war and victory
  • Ashokasundari - Goddess of freedom

Sacred Abodes

Shiva's primary abode is Mount Kailash in the Himalayas, where he meditates eternally. He is also present in countless temples across India and the world, particularly in the form of the sacred lingam.

Major Temples

  • Kedarnath - One of the 12 Jyotirlingas
  • Varanasi - The eternal city of Shiva
  • Amarnath - The sacred cave temple
  • Rameshwaram - Southern Jyotirlinga
  • Mount Kailash - His eternal abode

Philosophical Teachings

Shiva's teachings emphasize the importance of self-realization, meditation, and the understanding that the individual soul (atman) is one with the universal consciousness (Brahman).

Key Principles

  • Non-duality (Advaita)
  • Detachment from material world
  • Meditation and self-inquiry
  • Surrender to divine will
  • Service to all beings

Sacred Wisdom

"I am neither the body, nor the mind, nor the intellect. I am the eternal witness, the pure consciousness that pervades all."
- Lord Shiva
"The greatest meditation is a mind that lets go. The greatest wisdom is seeing through appearances."
- Lord Shiva
"In the midst of movement and chaos, keep stillness inside of you. That is the secret of yoga."
- Lord Shiva
