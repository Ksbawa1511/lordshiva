Amarnath Temple - The Sacred Cave of Lord Shiva

Discover the divine abode of Mahadev in the pristine Himalayas, where the natural ice lingam forms every year, symbolizing the eternal presence of Lord Shiva.

About Amarnath Temple

Amarnath Temple is a Hindu shrine located in Anantnag district of Jammu and Kashmir, India. A cave situated at an altitude of 3,888 m (12,756 ft), about 168 km from Anantnag city, the district headquarters, 141 km (88 mi) from Srinagar, the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, reached through Pahalgam town.

The shrine represents an important part of Hinduism. The cave, located in Lidder Valley, is surrounded by glaciers, snowy mountains and is covered with snow most of the year, except for a short period in the summer, when it is open to pilgrims.

The Sacred Shiva Linga

The Shiva Lingam at the shrine is a Swayambhu lingam. The lingam is a natural stalagmite formation inside a 40 m (130 ft) tall cave at an elevation of 3,888 m (12,756 ft) on the Amarnath Mountain, which has a peak of 5,186 metres (17,014 ft).

The stalagmite is formed due to the freezing of water drops that fall from the roof of the cave onto the floor, resulting in an upward growth of an ice formation. Here, the stalagmites considered as the lingam, a physical manifestation of Shiva, form a solid-dome-shape. Two smaller stalagmites are thought to represent Parvati and Ganesha.

According to the ancient Hindu texts of the Mahabharata and Puranas, a lingam represents Shiva. The lingam waxes during May to August, as snow melts in the Himalayas above the cave, and water seeps into the rocks of the cave; thereafter, the lingam gradually wanes.

Historical Significance

Ancient History

The book Rajatarangini (Book VII v. 183) refers to Krishaanth or Amarnath. It is believed that in the 11th century CE, Queen Suryamati gifted trishulas, banalingas and other sacred emblems to this temple. Rajavalipataka, begun by Prajna Bhatta, contains detailed references to the pilgrimage to Amarnath Cave Temple.

Medieval History

The cave and the Shivling finds mention in Abu'l Fazl's 16th century work Ain-i-Akbari. According to him, the site attracted many pilgrims. The Sacred Journey - Amarnath Yatra

The annual pilgrimage to Amarnath is one of the most sacred journeys in Hinduism. The yatra typically takes place during the months of July and August, coinciding with the Hindu month of Shravan. Pilgrims undertake this challenging journey through treacherous mountain paths, braving extreme weather conditions to seek the divine blessings of Lord Shiva.

The journey begins from Pahalgam or Baltal, with pilgrims walking through beautiful valleys, crossing rivers, and climbing steep mountain passes. The route is dotted with langars (community kitchens) and medical camps to support the pilgrims.

Spiritual Significance

Amarnath holds immense spiritual significance as it is believed to be the place where Lord Shiva revealed the secret of immortality (Amar Katha) to Goddess Parvati. The cave represents the eternal nature of Shiva and the cycle of creation and destruction.

According to legend, when Lord Shiva was narrating the secret of immortality to Parvati, a pair of doves overheard the conversation. These doves are said to have become immortal and can still be seen in the cave, symbolizing the eternal nature of the knowledge imparted here.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Amarnath Temple is during the annual yatra period, which usually falls between July and August. The cave is accessible only during this time when the snow melts and the path becomes navigable. The weather during this period is relatively mild, though still challenging due to the high altitude.

It's important to note that the yatra is subject to weather conditions and security considerations. Pilgrims are advised to check the official announcements and register for the yatra through the official channels.

How to Reach

By Air

The nearest airport is Srinagar International Airport, which is well-connected to major cities in India. From Srinagar, you can take a road journey to Pahalgam or Baltal.

By Road

Pahalgam and Baltal are the two base camps for the Amarnath Yatra. Both are accessible by road from Srinagar. Regular bus and taxi services are available from Srinagar to these base camps.

By Train

The nearest railway station is Jammu Tawi, which is well-connected to major cities. From Jammu, you can take a road journey to Srinagar and then proceed to the base camps.

Important Information for Pilgrims

  • Registration is mandatory for the yatra
  • Medical fitness certificate is required
  • Carry warm clothing and essential supplies
  • Follow the designated route and safety guidelines
  • Respect the environment and local customs
  • Be prepared for challenging weather conditions
